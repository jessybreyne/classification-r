Robbie Wins Again

Robbie “nob-head” Williams, who manages to come first in virtually every “100 Greatest …” thing going, has come tops in a survey of people’s preferred funeral songs with the song Angels. I can understand why people associate this song with funerals, I know every time I hear it I think of drinking bleach, but why would you want to associate such a truly awful song with the death of someone you care about? I suppose you might as well make an shite song too painful to listen to rather than ruining a classic.

Monday, March 07, 2005

First Impressions

“Hi, I’ve slept in your bed ”

This is how someone introduced themselves to me at a party I was at on Saturday. I’d never met them before in my life, and so felt a little confused.

“You weren’t there at the time”

Being pretty sure that I’ve never had a gay experience, I felt this clarification was a little unnecessary.

I eventually discovered that they’d visited one of my housemates during my second year at uni and had slept in my bed as I was away at the time. I think this has to be one of the weirdest intros I’ve ever had to someone.

Anything You Can Do...

If you though the Queen's comments posted yesterday were daft, check out some of these from her husband the Duke of Edinburgh:

Pointing to a fuse box…
- “It looks as though it was put in by an Indian.”

To a member of the British Deaf Association at a celebration…
- “If you are standing near there [the music], no wonder you are deaf.”

To a Scottish driving instructor…
- “How do you keep the natives off the booze long enough to pass the test?”

During a trip to Canada…
- “We don’t come here for our health. We can think of other ways of enjoying ourselves.”

To British students in China…
- “If you stay here much longer you’ll all be slitty-eyed.”

Tuesday, March 01, 2005

Advanced Warning

Faithless unleash their ‘greatest hits’ album Forever Faithless in April. I use the inverted commas because Faithless releasing a greatest hits album is like Michael Jackson releasing a best of home video, its just something no-one needs. To be fair though, Faithless have to be considered pioneers, few in music have done more to redefine the word shit. Just when you’re thinking “Rollo, Sister Bliss, you’ve nailed it there, that is without a shadow of doubt, shite” they go and surpass all your expectations.

Monday, February 28, 2005

H5N1

A combination of sub-arctic conditions and the flu meant that I didn’t get the chance to buy an album last week, so the album every week thing kind of died.

I felt pretty rough with the flu, thought the chicken flu pandemic may have started, but then realised that you can’t have a pandemic in this day and age with such a rubbish name. You only need to start worrying about chicken flu when someone gives it a new, scary name. I mean you can imagine a pandemic of something like ebola virus, but chicken flu, not going to happen.
