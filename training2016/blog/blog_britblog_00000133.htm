Lies, Damn Lies and Statistics (or Beware When You Go To The Polls)

Mucho kudos to Ankle Biting Pundits for exposing another fraudulent, anti-Bush poll (by the AP), this one regarding President Bush's Social Security Plan. They have done previous good work in exposing the fallacy of other poll results here (by the NYT) and here (by the MSM).

Please, read these posts in detail to understand how the media can mislead the public by not reporting the characteristics of the data upon which the poll numbers are based. Whether this approach is sinister or just plain sloppy reporting is hard to say. I work with statistical data all of the time, and I'm always reluctant to release any data analysis results without a full, detailed and documented disclosure of how the data was collected, analyzed and interpreted. Without this underlying analysis, there are too many people who are willing to take only the summarized results and spin them to suit their own interpretation and agenda.

Regarding poll results, no matter who reports them, you should always investigate to find out the following information, at a minimum:
  • Who conducted the poll (and is there any reason to believe they might be biased or have a hidden agenda)
  • The number of people participating in the poll
  • How the samples are selected (samples must always be selected at random in order for the results to be unbiased - you shouldn't collect data only from angry young men to assess exit polls)
  • Where the samples are selected from (selecting all samples randomly from NYC is really not a random sample, given the liberal tendencies of NYC)
  • The percent distribution of the sample demographics, which should reasonably reflect the percent distribution of the population demographics in order for the results of the poll to be valid. If they don't reasonably match, then the poll results are biased - find out why! By demographics, I mean things like age, gender, race, Democrat vs. Republican, Liberal vs. Conservative, registered voter vs. unregistered voter, geographic location, income levels, education levels, religion, marital status, etc.

The casual observer needs to understand that the intentional or unintentional manipulation of any of these demographics will provide poll results that are not representative of the opinions of the population. It's important that bloggers expose lies. It's critical that bloggers expose damn lies. It's imperative that bloggers expose the lies and damn lies that are fed to us through the unscrupulous use and reporting of statistics.
