Two more days!

I only have two more days until I am finished with classes. Today and tomorrow. Once I get through those - I'm done! It's really strange coming to the end of this chapter of my life. I can't wait.

I bought a really nice cream colored loveseat and chair-and-a-half set last week. Well, to be truthful, my father paid for it, and I am in process of buying it from him. The basement should look completely different when we get done with it. The new furniture set, getting rid of the old furniture, putting down a nice new grey-blue carpet. It will be much more homey.

Things with B are still going very well. I spent a really good chunk of time with him on Sunday after I finished work and before I had to go back. I got to meet his fuzzy children. Apparently, everyone but the chincilla like me. And I'm not too sure about the chincilla. He seemed okay with me being there until we left him alone for a little while. Then he got pissed off. Maybe he's not so much upset with me being there as with not being the center of attention. *smile* Anyway - all three animals (2 cats and the chin) are adorable. For some reason, every time I look at Popcorn, the chincilla, I hear the Taco Bell chihauhau talking in my head about leading a a revolution. Weird.

Aside from that - I talked to my dad about meeting B on Saturday. B is kind of nervous - he's expecting an inquisition. I mentioned that nobody expects the Spanish Inquisition and much hilarity ensued. I know - we are two geeks in a pod. *smile* It's really fun to be with someone that you have so much in common with.

Thursday, March 10, 2005

Movie Review - "Constantine"

Okay, this movie is getting horrid reviews, but I actually really enjoyed it. And that's aside from the fact that I saw it with B. *smile* I enjoy anything that takes a different view of Christian ideology, and this one certainly had it's own way of looking at this.

One of the problems a lot of people had with the movie was the casting of Keanu Reeves as Constantine. I actually thought that he did a very good job. From the set up of the character, you definitely don't want to see a smily, happy person running around the place. Reeves' brooding was not what you normally see from him, but was well done in my opinion.

I enjoyed seeing Rachel Weisz as she's a favorite actress of mine, but quite honestly, only one person in the cast (in my opinion) was irreplacable. Tilda Swinton was _awesome_. I really enjoyed her portrayal of... well, I don't want to spoil it for anyone else that wants to see it. Suffice it to say, I am _definitely_ looking forward to seeing how she handles the role of The White Witch in the newest incarnation of the Narnia Chronicles.

All in all, this was a good movie. I'm glad that I've never read the Hellblazer comics that it's based on. It sounds like I would probably be disappointed in the movie if I had already been a fan. But I might take a look into them now.

Monday, March 07, 2005

A big flashing neon sign for society

While checking out the news from outside the US today - something I do on a regular basis because it's nice to have an alternative view from time to time, I came across an article that completely disturbed me.

The gist of the article was this: researcher in Australia did a study on body image, self-esteem, and eating disorders. The research discovered that girls as young as _6_ years old were already considering themselves fat and thinking that they needed to be thinner.

What kind of sick, self-destructive, image and look obsessed world do we live in when children barely old enough to read a book are telling themselves that they are too fat and need to drop a few pounds?!?

I hate humanity and our society for telling people so many times that they aren't good enough if they don't fit into the size 2 box Madison Ave (and others of the same ilk) deem to be the ultimate in perfection.

I am not skinny. I am, to be quite frank, a fat chick. But you know what? I am usually pretty happy with myself. I have a great personality, a great sense of humor, and when I want to, I actually can look quite pretty. I don't really _like_ my size because I know it's unhealthy, but I can change that when I want to - if I want to.

I've had it. People suck - and yes, that's in a bad way. Every now and again you will find a gem of a person that doesn't (or at lest sucks in a good way *smile*), but they are so few and far between at times that I feel _entirely_ comfortable making my broad and sweeping statement.

Results of the informal poll and more

Well, I actually got a couple of responses on the informal poll of a couple of days ago. Good advice and thanks to everyone that participated. *smile* I also talked to B - the guy in question - to get his opinion on the whole thing since he opted not to leave a comment on the 'blog.

So, here's the decision. I am going to be true to my original intention with the 'blog and not censor myself by not including him in the writings. As with most of the people that I write about, he's not going to be mentioned by complete name, and I will not write anything on here that I wouldn't be comfortable saying directly to him. I also will not be writing about anything of an extremely personal nature. (Yeah, yeah, I know... I'm cutting out all the good stuff. Deal. *grin*)

It's going to be interesting to see how this all works out. For instance, when I am at work and filling time by 'blogging, I will sometimes have a mind ramble that I don't really conciously think about again after putting virtual pen to virtual paper.

But such is life. So, with that in mind, here's the scoop:

Not too long ago, I put up an ad on Yahoo! personals thinking that if nothing else, I might be able to make a new friend or two. Regardless of how it may look, I am not desperate for companionship, and was simply looking for a way to meet new people. Anyway - a little bit after I posted my ad, I got an email from B.

We started talking a bit through email and progressed to chatting on instant messenger programs. After a bit, we discovered that we have remarkably similar view points on a lot of things and decided to keep talking. I gave him the phone number for my VoIP phone and we started talking on the phone as well. Things going well to that point, we decided to meet (this hasn't occured yet - the first offical date is set for Wednesday of this week). So we'll see how that goes.

B is a really great guy. It's scary at times how similar we seem to be - very similar sense of humour, similar ideas on religion (or lack there of), similar ideas on children, etc. And you know that old saying about opposites attracting? I think the similar people tend to have a way better time of it. I really enjoy talking to him. He has this tendency to say things that make me blush (a hard feat in and of itself).

In other news - another friend of mine, S from Australia, whom I haven't talked to in forever was finally available at the same time as me today. I really should have been sleeping, but the damn sun came out again and woke me up. Anyway, during the course of our conversation, S jokingly called me a spinster.

Now, I have absolutely no interest in spinning, so I can only assume that he was refering to the other, more well-known definition: an elderly unmarried woman. Well, I immediately took exception to that. I mean, I'm only 29 for crying out loud. (I think this was his way of getting back at me for asking if he was 40 yet even though I know perfectly well how old he is) So I looked up the definition on Dictionary.com. Their definition is "a woman who has remained single beyond the conventional age for marrying." I suppose that is actually me. I have made a choice to remain unmarried and I am well beyond the "conventional" age for having gotten married.

So, I accept it. I am a spinster. Now to learn how to revel in my spinsterhood.

An informal poll...

Well, I've hit a bit of a quandry. I met a guy online not long ago, and I'm trying to decide whether or not to start including him in my 'blog or not. On the one hand, it's really relevent to my life and does tend to occupy the ramblings of my mind on a rather frequent basis. On the other hand, he reads my 'blog with fair regularity.

So, do I 'blog and give him a glimpse into thoughts that he might not otherwise know? Or do I keep quiet and not include that side of my life in my 'blog? I look at this 'blog as an outlet for my sometimes chaotic life. So if I want to be true to that, I know that I should include him. But a girl does have to have some secrets, right? *smile*

This isn't really a pressing issue. I have a feeling that if I started to include him in the 'blog and specifically told him about posts I didn't want him to read, he would respect that. But I don't want to start censoring what people read about on here - not that it's always that interesting to begin with.

Let's take a poll. I know that there are at least a few people that visit here on a regular basis. That stealthy little Blogpatrol counter is pretty cool. So, drop a comment or an email and let me know what you think. I promise not to bow to peer pressure, but I will take it into account. *smile*
